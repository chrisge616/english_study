## 🔑 Vocabulary

### High Priority

- Word: nuance
  - Meaning (simple English): a small but important difference
  - Example: There is an important nuance in that sentence.

- Word: pivotal
  - Meaning (simple English): serving as a turning point
  - Example: That meeting was pivotal for the project.

- Word: transformative
  - Meaning (simple English): causing major positive change
  - Example: Reading deeply can be transformative.

- Word: significant
  - Meaning (simple English): important in a meaningful way
  - Example: The change had a significant effect.

- Word: distill
  - Meaning (simple English): to reduce something to its essential parts
  - Example: We need to distill the idea clearly.

- Word: deviate
  - Meaning (simple English): to move away from a path or standard
  - Example: Do not deviate from the main point.

- Word: strategy
  - Meaning (simple English): a long-term plan
  - Example: We need a strategy before we act.

- Word: tactic
  - Meaning (simple English): a short-term method or move
  - Example: That was a smart tactic.

- Word: maneuvering
  - Meaning (simple English): making skillful moves in the moment
  - Example: He is good at maneuvering through tense situations.

- Word: excruciating
  - Meaning (simple English): extremely painful
  - Example: The injury caused excruciating pain.


### Medium Priority

- Word: reinforce
  - Meaning (simple English): to make something stronger
  - Example: Repetition helps reinforce memory.

- Word: iterate
  - Meaning (simple English): to repeat and improve
  - Example: We iterate on the design every week.

- Word: burden
  - Meaning (simple English): something heavy to carry or deal with
  - Example: That responsibility became a burden.

- Word: envision
  - Meaning (simple English): to imagine something clearly
  - Example: I can envision a better system.

- Word: fountain of ideas
  - Meaning (simple English): a continuous flow of ideas
  - Example: She is a fountain of ideas.

- Word: encyclopedia
  - Meaning (simple English): a collection of knowledge across many topics
  - Example: This book feels like an encyclopedia.

- Word: lay of the land
  - Meaning (simple English): the overall situation or structure
  - Example: First, get the lay of the land.

- Word: semantic tree
  - Meaning (simple English): a structure that organizes meanings from general to specific
  - Example: A semantic tree helps organize ideas.

- Word: lopsided
  - Meaning (simple English): very uneven
  - Example: The discussion became lopsided.

- Word: cohesively
  - Meaning (simple English): in a well-connected way
  - Example: The essay was written cohesively.


### Low Priority

- Word: conventionally
  - Meaning (simple English): in the usual or traditional way
  - Example: The problem is conventionally explained.

- Word: rally
  - Meaning (simple English): to gather support or recover strongly
  - Example: The team rallied after losing early points.

- Word: concord
  - Meaning (simple English): agreement or harmony
  - Example: The groups worked in concord.

- Word: metallurgy
  - Meaning (simple English): the study and processing of metals
  - Example: He became interested in metallurgy.

## ⚠️ Mistakes & Corrections

❌ "excruciating wait"  
✅ Use "excruciating" only for very intense pain or suffering.

❌ "dock the volume"  
✅ "dock" is used for deductions like pay, points, or score.

❌ "I’m trying to distillation this code"  
✅ I’m trying to distill this code.

❌ "It’s significant I drank coffee"  
✅ Use "significant" only when the impact is meaningful.

❌ nuance = deep meaning  
✅ nuance = a small difference that changes the feeling or interpretation.

- Tactic = specific method
- Maneuvering = real-time adjustment
- Pivotal = a turning point
- Transformative = deep, lasting change
- Deviate = formal movement away from a path
